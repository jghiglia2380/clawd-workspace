# Chapter 6: The Fish Auction

## Metadata
- **Season:** 2
- **Chapter:** 6
- **Title:** The Fish Auction
- **Type:** Memory — Mr. Mason
- **Setting:** Frame: Mr. Mason's attic | Memory: Japanese fish market (Tsukiji-style)
- **FL Concept:** Auctions, bidding, competition determines price
- **Character Beat:** Ellis gains confidence — his analytical thinking helps the group

## Story Summary
Mr. Mason tells the story of witnessing a tuna auction in Japan. He watched buyers compete, prices rise, and learned that in an auction, the BUYERS set the price through competition — not the seller. Ellis, in the frame story, makes analytical observations that help the group understand the lesson, earning recognition for his thinking.

**Setting:** Attic, around the trunk

**Action:** Mr. Mason reaches into the trunk and pulls out the small carved wooden fish. "This one is from Japan. The fish market. Have you ever heard of an auction?"

**Dialogue Seed:** "In Morocco, I learned to negotiate. In Japan, I learned something different — I learned about auctions."

**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason finding a viewing spot, buyers gathering

**Key Visual:** Auction area with numbered tuna, serious buyers gathering, tension building

**Setting:** Auction area
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason watching, auctioneer, buyers
**Action:** An auctioneer steps up and begins speaking rapidly in Japanese. Young Mr. Mason can't understand the words, but watches the buyers' hands.

### Scene 6: Hands Go Up
**Setting:** Auction area

**Action:** Buyers raise hands, making quick signals. The auctioneer responds. Numbers climb. Young Mr. Mason realizes: they're bidding. Competing.
**Key Visual:** Multiple hands raised, fingers showing numbers, rapid exchange
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "They were fighting — not with fists, but with prices. Whoever wanted that fish most would have to prove it."

---

### Scene 7: The Price Climbs
**Setting:** Auction area
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason watching, two buyers in fierce competition
**Action:** Two buyers go back and forth on one particularly beautiful tuna. The price keeps climbing higher and higher.
**Key Visual:** Two buyers locked in competition, others watching, price rising
**Dialogue Seed:** "Higher. Higher. I couldn't believe how much one fish could cost."

---

### Scene 8: Someone Wins
**Setting:** Auction area
**Characters:** Winning buyer, auctioneer
**Action:** One buyer finally wins. The other backs down. A bell rings. The tuna is marked with the winner's number. Applause.
**Key Visual:** Winner bowing slightly, tuna being marked, moment of resolution
**Dialogue Seed:** "And just like that — sold. One fish, worth thousands. Because one person wanted it more than anyone else."

---

### Scene 9: Talking to a Buyer
**Setting:** Edge of auction area
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, friendly fish buyer
**Action:** Young Mr. Mason approaches a buyer who didn't win. The man explains how auctions work in simple terms.
**Key Visual:** Conversation at the edge of the action, friendly local explaining
**Dialogue Seed:** "I asked him: 'Who decides the price?' He laughed. 'We do. The buyers.'"

---

### Scene 10: The Key Lesson
**Setting:** Same
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, the buyer
**Action:** The buyer explains: in a negotiation, buyer and seller agree. In an auction, BUYERS compete against EACH OTHER. The seller just watches.
**Key Visual:** The buyer gesturing between himself and other buyers
**Dialogue Seed:** "The seller doesn't set the price. The buyers do — by competing. Whoever wants it most, pays the most."

---

### Scene 11: Understanding Competition
**Setting:** Walking through the market
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason
**Action:** Young Mr. Mason walks through the market with new eyes. He sees how competition affects prices everywhere — more buyers for less fish means higher prices.
**Key Visual:** Young man observing the market thoughtfully, gears turning
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "I started to see it everywhere. When people compete, prices go up. When there's plenty, prices go down."

---

## FL Vocabulary to Include

- **Auction:** A sale where buyers compete by offering higher prices
- **Bid:** To offer a price in an auction
- **Competition:** When multiple people try to get the same thing
- **Demand:** How much people want something
