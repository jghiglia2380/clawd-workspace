# Chapter 5: The Spice Market

## Metadata
- **Season:** 2
- **Chapter:** 5
- **Title:** The Spice Market
- **Type:** Memory — Mr. Mason
- **Setting:** Frame: Mr. Mason's attic | Memory: Moroccan souk
- **FL Concept:** Negotiation, fair pricing, patience in commerce
- **Character Beat:** Layla learns patience — "Maybe I need to slow down and listen more"

## Story Summary
Mr. Mason tells the story of his first visit to a Moroccan spice market as a young man. He wanted to buy saffron for his mother but didn't know how to haggle. A patient merchant taught him that negotiation is a conversation, not a battle — and that rushing ruins deals. In the frame story, Layla recognizes her own tendency to rush and resolves to practice patience.

**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, Hassan (patient older merchant)

### Scene 9: Starting the Conversation
**Setting:** Hassan's stall
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, Hassan
**Action:** Young Mr. Mason explains he wants saffron for his mother. Hassan listens carefully, asks questions about what she'll use it for.
**Key Visual:** Two people in genuine conversation, Hassan listening intently

**Setting:** Same stall

### Scene 11: The Price Discussion

### Scene 12: Learning to Negotiate
**Setting:** Same stall
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, Hassan
**Action:** Hassan explains that negotiation isn't fighting — it's a conversation. "Tell me what you can pay. I'll tell you what I can accept. We'll find where we both smile."

**Dialogue Seed:** "A good deal is when both people walk away happy. Not just one."

---

### Scene 13: Finding the Middle
**Setting:** Same stall
**Characters:** Young Mr. Mason, Hassan
**Action:** They negotiate respectfully. Young Mr. Mason offers less, Hassan comes down a little. They find a middle ground. Both smile.

**Dialogue Seed:** "This price works for you?" "It does." "Then it works for me too. We both win."

### Scene 14: The Purchase

## FL Vocabulary to Include

- **Negotiate:** Talk to agree on a fair price
- **Fair price:** A price that makes both buyer and seller happy
- **Patience:** Waiting calmly without rushing
- **Value:** What something is worth to someone

## Activity Tie-In

**Role-Play Haggling**
Students practice negotiation in pairs — one buyer, one seller — trying to find a price that makes both happy. Reflect on how it felt to listen vs. to rush.
