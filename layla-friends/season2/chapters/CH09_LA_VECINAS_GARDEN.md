# Chapter 9: La Vecina's Garden

## Metadata
- **Season:** 2
- **Chapter:** 9
- **Title:** La Vecina's Garden
- **Type:** Memory — Frances
- **Setting:** Frame: Neighborhood (Frances's porch) | Memory: Frances's childhood neighborhood
- **FL Concept:** Local economy, community commerce, knowing your seller
- **S1 Callback:** Frances mentions her seed library from Season 1
- **Wisdom Transfer:** Frances passes on what she learned

## Story Summary
The scene shifts to Frances. She invites the friends to her garden and tells them about Doña Carmen — a neighbor from her childhood who sold vegetables from her front yard. Frances learned about trust, community, and the value of knowing where your food comes from. This chapter introduces Frances as a wisdom-keeper alongside Mr. Mason.

## Scene-by-Scene Outline

### Scene 1: Visiting Frances
**Setting:** Walking to Frances's house
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** After several days of Mr. Mason's stories, the friends visit Frances. They want to help in her garden and ask about her seed library.
**Key Visual:** Friends walking down sunny street toward Frances's house
**Dialogue Seed:** Layla: "Frances might have stories too. She's lived here a long time." Riley: "And we should check on our seeds from the seed library!"

---

### Scene 2: Frances's Garden
**Setting:** Frances's garden, lush and green
**Characters:** Friends arriving, Frances working in the garden
**Action:** They find Frances in her garden, surrounded by tomatoes, peppers, and herbs. She looks up with her warm smile.
**Key Visual:** Beautiful garden, Frances with dirt on her hands, welcoming expression
**Dialogue Seed:** "Well hello! Come to check on your plants? Or to dig in the dirt?"

---

### Scene 3: Seeds Growing
**Setting:** Garden bed area
**Characters:** Friends, Frances showing them plants
**Action:** Frances shows them the seeds they planted in Season 1 — now they're growing strong. She ties this to the lessons about patience.
**Key Visual:** Small plants growing, friends amazed at the progress
**Dialogue Seed:** "Remember when these were tiny seeds? Patience and care. Now look."
**S1 Callback:** Direct reference to the seed library episode.

---

### Scene 4: The Question
**Setting:** Garden, sitting on a bench
**Characters:** Friends, Frances
**Action:** The friends tell Frances about their Community Market idea and Mr. Mason's stories. They ask: "Do you have stories about markets too?"
**Key Visual:** Friends gathered around Frances, hopeful expressions
**Dialogue Seed:** "Mr. Mason showed us markets around the world. Do you have stories about buying and selling?"

---

### Scene 5: Frances Smiles
**Setting:** Garden bench
**Characters:** Frances with a memory stirring
**Action:** Frances gets a faraway look — the look Mr. Mason gets when he's about to tell a story. "I do. Let me tell you about Doña Carmen."
**Key Visual:** Frances's expression softening with memory
**Dialogue Seed:** "I didn't travel far like Mr. Mason. But I learned just as much from a woman who lived three houses down."

---

### Scene 6: Transition to Memory
**Setting:** Frances's garden fading to her childhood neighborhood
**Characters:** Visual transformation
**Action:** The garden shimmers. We transition to a neighborhood from Frances's childhood — similar houses, older cars, a different era.
**Key Visual:** Soft transition, colors slightly different (warmer, more vintage)
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "When I was a little girl, my neighborhood looked a lot like this one. And the best vegetable stand wasn't in any store."

---

### Scene 7: Young Frances
**Setting:** Childhood neighborhood street
**Characters:** Young Frances (about 8 years old)
**Action:** Young Frances walks down the street with coins in her pocket. Her mother has sent her on an errand.
**Key Visual:** Young girl walking on sidewalk, carrying a small coin purse
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "Every Saturday, Mamá would give me some coins and say: 'Go to Doña Carmen's. Get us tomatoes.'"

---

### Scene 8: Doña Carmen's Yard
**Setting:** A neighbor's front yard with a small vegetable display
**Characters:** Young Frances approaching, Doña Carmen visible
**Action:** Young Frances arrives at a house with a small table in the front yard — tomatoes, peppers, squash, herbs laid out simply.
**Key Visual:** Modest but beautiful vegetable stand in a front yard, Doña Carmen arranging items
**Dialogue Seed:** "Doña Carmen grew everything herself. Right there in her backyard. She'd set out a little table every morning."

---

### Scene 9: Doña Carmen
**Setting:** The vegetable stand
**Characters:** Young Frances, Doña Carmen (elderly Latina woman)
**Action:** Doña Carmen greets young Frances warmly by name. She knows everyone in the neighborhood. She asks about Frances's family.
**Key Visual:** Kind elderly woman in apron, genuine warmth, personal connection
**Dialogue Seed:** "Hola, Francesita! How is your mamá? How is school? Tell me everything."

---

### Scene 10: Choosing Tomatoes
**Setting:** Same stand
**Characters:** Young Frances, Doña Carmen
**Action:** Doña Carmen helps young Frances pick the best tomatoes, pointing out which are ripest. She explains how to tell a good one.
**Key Visual:** Two sets of hands over tomatoes, teaching moment
**Dialogue Seed:** "Feel this one. See how it gives just a little? That's ready. That one needs another day."

---

### Scene 11: The Extra Gift
**Setting:** Same stand
**Characters:** Young Frances, Doña Carmen
**Action:** After Frances pays, Doña Carmen adds an extra handful of peppers. "For your mamá. Tell her hello." Always something extra for neighbors.
**Key Visual:** Doña Carmen adding peppers to the bag, generous gesture
**Dialogue Seed:** "You don't need to pay for these. We're neighbors. This is how it works."

---

### Scene 12: Knowing Where It Comes From
**Setting:** Walking home, memory
**Characters:** Young Frances walking, looking at the bag
**Action:** Young Frances walks home. She can see Doña Carmen's garden from the street. She knows exactly where her food grew.
**Key Visual:** Young girl looking back at the garden visible behind the house
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "I could point to the exact spot where those tomatoes grew. I knew who planted them, who watered them, who picked them. I knew."

---

### Scene 13: Trust and Connection
**Setting:** Memory, slightly faded
**Characters:** Young Frances arriving home
**Action:** Frances thinks about how different this is from a big store. At the store, you don't know anyone. At Doña Carmen's, you're family.
**Key Visual:** Young Frances entering her home, happy
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "Mamá never worried about Doña Carmen's vegetables. She trusted her. That trust was worth more than any price."

---

### Scene 14: Transition Back
**Setting:** Childhood fading to Frances's current garden
**Characters:** Visual transformation
**Action:** The childhood memory fades. Colors shift back to present. Frances is on her garden bench, friends listening.
**Key Visual:** Soft return to present
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "That's where I learned that a market can be more than a place. It can be a relationship."

---

### Scene 15: Frances's Wisdom
**Setting:** Frances's garden, present
**Characters:** Frances, all four friends
**Action:** Frances looks at the friends. "Doña Carmen taught me about growing and sharing. I've tried to do the same with my seed library. Now I'm teaching you."
**Key Visual:** Frances with loving expression, connecting generations
**Dialogue Seed:** "Someday, you'll teach someone else. That's how it works."
**Wisdom Transfer:** Explicit passing of knowledge.

---

### Scene 16: Knowing Your Seller
**Setting:** Garden
**Characters:** Friends discussing
**Action:** Ellis asks what made Doña Carmen's stand special. Frances says: "You knew her. You trusted her. She cared about what she sold because she cared about YOU."
**Key Visual:** Friends absorbing this
**Dialogue Seed:** "When you know who makes your food, you're not just a customer. You're part of their story."

---

### Scene 17: For the Community Market
**Setting:** Garden
**Characters:** Friends
**Action:** The friends realize: for their market, they should know the people selling. It should feel like neighbors, not strangers.
**Key Visual:** Friends nodding, making connections
**Dialogue Seed:** Layla: "Our market should feel like this. Like a neighborhood. Where everyone knows each other."

---

### Scene 18: Design Your Own Stand
**Setting:** Garden
**Characters:** Friends, Frances
**Action:** Frances suggests the friends think about what THEY would sell at a stand. What would they grow, make, share? How would they treat their neighbors?
**Key Visual:** Frances gesturing encouragingly
**Dialogue Seed:** "If you had your own little stand, what would you offer? Think about it."

---

### Scene 19: Planning Together
**Setting:** Garden
**Characters:** Friends discussing excitedly
**Action:** The friends start imagining their stands: Riley would offer art, Ellis would organize and track, Benny would help carry, Layla would... she's not sure yet.
**Key Visual:** Friends brainstorming, garden background
**Dialogue Seed:** "I'd definitely do art!" "I could help organize everything!" "I'm good at carrying heavy stuff!"

---

### Scene 20: More Stories to Come
**Setting:** Garden
**Characters:** Frances, friends
**Action:** Frances mentions she has more memories to share — a German Christmas market she once visited, and a tradition from her family in Mexico. The friends are excited.
**Key Visual:** Frances with hints of more to tell, friends eager
**Dialogue Seed:** "But those are stories for another day. One at a time, mijos. Like good tomatoes ripening."

## FL Vocabulary to Include

- **Local:** From nearby, from your own community
- **Trust:** Believing someone will be honest and fair
- **Relationship:** A connection between people who know each other
- **Community:** A group of people who live near each other and help each other
