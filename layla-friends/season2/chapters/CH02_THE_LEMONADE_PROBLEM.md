# Chapter 2: The Lemonade Problem

## Metadata
- **Season:** 2
- **Chapter:** 2
- **Title:** The Lemonade Problem
- **Type:** Neighborhood (Present)
- **Setting:** Layla's front yard and surrounding street
- **FL Concept:** Supply & demand — when there are too many sellers, prices drop
- **Character Beat:** Ellis notices the pattern first (analytical thinking emerging)

## Story Summary
It's a hot summer day, and Layla decides to set up a lemonade stand. But when she gets outside, she discovers Riley, Ellis, and even some other neighborhood kids have had the same idea. Too many lemonade stands on one street! Nobody's buying from anyone. The friends have to figure out why — and discover the concept of supply and demand.

## Scene-by-Scene Outline

### Scene 1: A Hot Day
**Setting:** Inside Layla's house, looking out the window
**Characters:** Layla
**Action:** Layla fans herself. It's really hot outside. She gets an idea — a lemonade stand would be perfect today.
**Key Visual:** Layla by the window, sun streaming in, wiping her forehead
**Dialogue Seed:** "It's so hot! Everyone must be thirsty. I should sell lemonade!"

---

### Scene 2: Making Lemonade
**Setting:** Layla's kitchen
**Characters:** Layla (parent in background optional)
**Action:** Layla squeezes lemons, adds sugar, stirs the pitcher. She's excited about her plan.
**Key Visual:** Layla at kitchen counter with lemons, pitcher, big smile
**Dialogue Seed:** "Fresh lemonade! Everyone's going to want some."

---

### Scene 3: Setting Up the Stand
**Setting:** Layla's front yard
**Characters:** Layla
**Action:** Layla carries a small table outside, sets up cups, makes a sign: "LEMONADE 50¢"
**Key Visual:** Layla arranging her stand, colorful sign, pitcher ready
**Dialogue Seed:** "Perfect! Now I just wait for customers."

---

### Scene 4: The First Surprise
**Setting:** View down the street from Layla's yard
**Characters:** Layla, looking down street
**Action:** Layla looks up and sees another lemonade stand a few houses down. It's Riley!
**Key Visual:** Layla's surprised face, Riley's stand visible in the distance
**Dialogue Seed:** "Wait... is that Riley? She has a stand too?"

---

### Scene 5: Even More Stands
**Setting:** Street view, wider shot
**Characters:** Layla walking, multiple stands visible
**Action:** Layla walks down the sidewalk and counts: Riley's stand, Ellis's stand, Benny's stand, and two more from other neighborhood kids. SIX lemonade stands on one street!
**Key Visual:** Street lined with lemonade stands, each with a kid behind it, Layla counting in disbelief
**Dialogue Seed:** "One, two, three... six lemonade stands?!"

---

### Scene 6: Nobody's Buying
**Setting:** Back at Layla's stand
**Characters:** Layla, sitting and waiting
**Action:** Layla sits behind her stand. A few people walk by but don't stop. She looks confused and a little worried.
**Key Visual:** Layla alone at stand, chin in hands, empty cup stack
**Dialogue Seed:** "Where is everyone? It's so hot — why isn't anyone buying?"

---

### Scene 7: Checking on Riley
**Setting:** Riley's lemonade stand
**Characters:** Layla approaching, Riley behind her stand
**Action:** Layla walks to Riley's stand. Riley looks just as puzzled — she hasn't sold anything either.
**Key Visual:** Two girls at Riley's stand, both looking confused
**Dialogue Seed:** "You haven't sold any either?" "Not one cup!"

---

### Scene 8: The Group Gathers
**Setting:** Midpoint of the street, between stands
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** Ellis and Benny join them. Everyone's had the same problem — lots of stands, zero sales.
**Key Visual:** Four friends standing together in the street, gesturing at the various stands around them
**Dialogue Seed:** Benny: "I thought everyone would be thirsty!" Ellis: "They probably are... but something's wrong."

---

### Scene 9: Ellis Thinks
**Setting:** Same location
**Characters:** Ellis, friends listening
**Action:** Ellis looks up and down the street, counting the stands. His analytical mind starts working.
**Key Visual:** Ellis with thoughtful expression, finger on chin, looking at all the stands
**Dialogue Seed:** "Let me think about this. Six stands. Maybe twenty people who walk by. That's not very many customers for each of us..."

---

### Scene 10: The Penny Drops
**Setting:** Same location
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** Ellis explains: when there are lots of sellers but not many buyers, nobody sells much. "There's too much lemonade and not enough thirsty people."
**Key Visual:** Ellis gesturing explanatorily, friends having "aha" expressions
**Dialogue Seed:** "When there's a lot of something, it's harder to sell. We're all competing for the same customers!"

---

### Scene 11: What About Price?
**Setting:** Same location, looking at a neighbor's stand
**Characters:** Friends, neighbor kid visible at their stand
**Action:** One of the other kids drops their price to 25¢, trying to get customers. Layla notices.
**Key Visual:** Neighbor kid changing their sign from 50¢ to 25¢
**Dialogue Seed:** "Look! They lowered their price. Now people might go there instead of our stands."

---

### Scene 12: The Race to the Bottom
**Setting:** Street, multiple stands visible
**Characters:** Various kids at stands
**Action:** Quick montage: another kid drops to 20¢, then another to 15¢. Everyone's lowering prices trying to compete.
**Key Visual:** Signs being changed, prices dropping: 25¢, 20¢, 15¢
**Dialogue Seed:** (Narration) "Soon everyone was lowering their prices, trying to get customers."

---

### Scene 13: This Isn't Working
**Setting:** Back with the four friends
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** The friends realize that lowering prices isn't helping — now everyone's selling lemonade for almost nothing, and STILL not many people are buying.
**Key Visual:** Friends looking frustrated, nearly empty tip jars
**Dialogue Seed:** Benny: "Now we're selling for almost nothing AND no one's buying!" Riley: "There has to be a better way."

---

### Scene 14: A Different Idea
**Setting:** Same location
**Characters:** Layla, friends
**Action:** Layla gets an idea. "What if we stopped competing and worked together instead? One stand, all of us."
**Key Visual:** Layla with lightbulb expression, friends looking interested
**Dialogue Seed:** "Instead of six tiny stands, what if there was ONE really good stand?"

---

### Scene 15: Combining Forces
**Setting:** Layla's front yard
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** The friends bring their supplies together — more lemons, more cups, a bigger table. They make one impressive stand.
**Key Visual:** Friends working together, combining pitchers and supplies, building a bigger setup
**Dialogue Seed:** "We can make more lemonade together than any of us could alone!"

---

### Scene 16: Making It Special
**Setting:** Same, the combined stand
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** Riley makes a beautiful new sign. Ellis organizes the supplies. Benny arranges the cups. Layla adds fresh lemon slices to the pitcher.
**Key Visual:** Each friend contributing their strength to the stand
**Dialogue Seed:** Riley: "A pretty sign will catch people's eyes!" Ellis: "And we can keep it organized so it's fast."

---

### Scene 17: The New Stand Opens
**Setting:** Layla's front yard, now with impressive stand
**Characters:** Friends behind stand, neighbors walking by
**Action:** The combined stand looks great — colorful sign, neat setup, plenty of lemonade. People start stopping to look.
**Key Visual:** Attractive stand with all four friends, passersby showing interest
**Dialogue Seed:** "Fresh lemonade! Made by friends, for friends!"

---

### Scene 18: Customers Arrive
**Setting:** Same
**Characters:** Friends serving customers
**Action:** People start buying. With less competition (other kids gave up or joined them) and a nicer stand, business is good.
**Key Visual:** Friends happily serving lemonade, customers smiling, cups being poured
**Dialogue Seed:** Customer: "This looks much better than those tiny stands!" Benny: "We're a team!"

---

### Scene 19: Reflecting on the Lesson
**Setting:** Same, end of day, stand closing up
**Characters:** All four friends, counting their earnings
**Action:** The friends count their coins. They made more together than they would have alone. Ellis explains what they learned.
**Key Visual:** Friends sitting together, small pile of coins, satisfied expressions
**Dialogue Seed:** Ellis: "When there's too much of something, prices go down and nobody wins. But when we combined, we became something special."

---

### Scene 20: Connecting to the Bigger Idea
**Setting:** Same
**Characters:** All four friends
**Action:** Layla connects this to their earlier idea: "For our Community Market, we shouldn't all sell the same thing. Everyone should bring something different!"
**Key Visual:** Friends nodding in agreement, sun setting in background
**Dialogue Seed:** "If we want our market to work, we need variety — not six lemonade stands!"

## FL Vocabulary to Include

- **Supply:** How much of something there is to sell
- **Demand:** How many people want to buy something
- **Competition:** When multiple sellers try to get the same customers
- **Price:** How much something costs

## Activity Tie-In

**Price-Setting Simulation**
Students simulate a market with too many sellers of the same item, watch prices drop, then discuss strategies (differentiation, combining, etc.)
