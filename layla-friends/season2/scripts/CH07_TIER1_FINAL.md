## THE FAMILY BAKERY

### Scene 1 (31 words)
Mr. Mason picked up a wooden stamp. It had a pretty pattern. "This came from Italy," he said. "From a tiny village. A bakery had been there for one hundred years."

### Scene 2 (30 words)
The attic faded away. Stone houses appeared. A narrow street with bumpy stones. Morning light came through. The air smelled like baking bread. It smelled so good! They were in Italy.

### Scene 3 (31 words)
Young Mr. Mason followed the smell. He walked through the small streets. He found a little bakery. A wooden sign said "Rossi" in old letters. Warm light came from inside.

### Scene 4 (30 words)
Inside the bakery, he saw three people. An old grandfather was making dough. His son was at the oven. A teenage boy was wrapping bread. Three family members working together!

### Scene 5 (31 words)
The grandfather's name was Nonno. He had made bread for sixty years. His hands moved in the dough slowly. They knew just what to do. He looked happy and calm.

### Scene 6 (30 words)
The father's name was Paolo. He used a long paddle. He put bread in the oven. He took bread out. The bread was golden and perfect. Paolo knew his oven well.

### Scene 7 (31 words)
The grandson's name was Marco. He was young like Mr. Mason. Marco wrapped the bread in paper. He talked to customers. He gave them change. He was learning the whole business.

### Scene 8 (30 words)
Marco saw young Mr. Mason watching. "You want to try?" Marco asked with a smile. "Come, I will show you!" Young Mr. Mason was surprised but happy to help.

### Scene 9 (31 words)
The grandfather taught young Mr. Mason to knead. He put his hands in the dough. It was hard! The old man helped him. "Not so hard," he said gently. "Be gentle."

### Scene 10 (30 words)
Young Mr. Mason tried to go fast. The grandfather stopped him. "Bread cannot be rushed," he said. "Good things take time." Young Mr. Mason slowed down. Now it was easier.

### Scene 11 (31 words)
Young Mr. Mason asked Paolo, "Why do you all make bread?" Paolo smiled. "My father taught me. I teach my son. We are keeping something alive. Something special for our family."

### Scene 12 (30 words)
Marco showed him the wooden stamp. They pressed it into every loaf. "This stamp is older than my grandfather," Marco said. "Every loaf carries our name. It is our promise."

### Scene 13 (31 words)
When young Mr. Mason left, the family gave him a gift. It was a bread stamp they had carved. "Remember us," they said. "Making things with your hands matters. It is special."

### Scene 14 (30 words)
The bakery faded away. Flour dust became attic dust. They were back. Mr. Mason held the stamp. It was the same one from the story. The memory lived on.

### Scene 15 (31 words)
"Three generations," said Mr. Mason. "Same bakery. Same love." He held the stamp close. The friends were quiet. They felt something warm. Family work was special. It connected people together.

### Scene 16 (30 words)
The friends thought about their own families. "My grandma paints," said Riley. "Maybe that is why I like art." Benny added, "My dad fixes things. He teaches me too."

### Scene 17 (31 words)
Ellis had an idea. "We should ask our families about their jobs!" he said. "Like an interview. What do they do? How did they learn?" Everyone thought that sounded great.

### Scene 18 (30 words)
Layla connected it to their market. "At our Community Market, people could teach things. Not just sell things. They could share what they know!" That was a wonderful new idea.

### Scene 19 (31 words)
Mr. Mason smiled proudly. "Now you are thinking well," he said. "A market is not just about stuff. It is about knowledge. It is about people helping each other learn."

### Scene 20 (31 words)
The friends felt excited. More stories waited in the trunk. Their Community Market was becoming something bigger. "What else is in there?" asked Layla. "We will see," said Mr. Mason. "One story at a time."
