**Target Word Count:** 1,170 words
**Episode Duration:** ~8 minutes
**Character Focus:** All four friends working together
**FL Integration:** Fair trade, perceived value — what's "junk" to one is treasure to another
**Setting:** Neighborhood community center

## THE SWAP MEET

### Scene 1 (58 words)
Layla walked through the neighborhood on a sunny Saturday morning, enjoying the fresh air and warm sunshine. She noticed something colorful attached to a telephone pole up ahead. As she got closer, she recognized it as a hand-painted sign with bright, cheerful letters. The sign looked homemade, which made her curious. She stopped walking to read what it said.

### Scene 2 (60 words)
"Community Swap Meet — Today!" the sign announced in bold letters. Below that, it explained: "Bring what you don't need. Find what you do!" Layla read the details about time and location — ten o'clock at the community center. Her eyes lit up with recognition and excitement. "That sounds just like our Trading Post, but so much bigger!" she said aloud with enthusiasm.

### Scene 3 (58 words)
Layla ran to Riley's house and knocked eagerly on the front door. Riley answered, still wearing her pajamas with her hair slightly messy. "You absolutely have to come see this!" Layla said breathlessly with excitement. She explained everything about the swap meet sign. Riley's face brightened with interest immediately. "A swap meet? We should definitely bring stuff to trade!"

### Scene 4 (61 words)
The two friends hurried to the park to find Ellis and Benny, who were tossing a ball back and forth near the swings. "Guess what's happening at the community center today?" Layla called out as they approached. She explained the swap meet concept with enthusiasm. Ellis looked genuinely interested in the idea. "What should we bring to trade?" he asked thoughtfully. Everyone started making plans together.

### Scene 5 (58 words)
Layla went home to search through her belongings carefully. She opened her closet door and sorted through her toy box with purpose. She discovered a puzzle she had completed many times before over the years. Every piece was familiar to her now, and she knew every corner by heart. "Maybe someone else would enjoy figuring this out," she decided.

### Scene 6 (59 words)
Riley looked through her collection of art supplies at home. She found several colored pencils that had gotten short from regular use. She also discovered stickers she no longer felt attached to keeping. "These pencils still work perfectly fine," she reasoned while examining them. "Someone else might really appreciate them." She placed everything carefully in a small canvas bag.

### Scene 7 (58 words)
Ellis organized his room before carefully selecting items to bring. He chose a book he had already read and enjoyed twice. He also found a toy car he had outgrown over time. He placed both items in a sturdy box. "I loved this book when I first read it," he thought. "It's time for someone else to discover it."

### Scene 8 (59 words)
Benny searched through his belongings with his usual enthusiasm and energy. He found a bright bouncy ball and a small toy drum buried in his closet. He hadn't played with either in quite a long time. "I completely forgot I even had these!" he said cheerfully to himself. He picked them up happily and added them to his pile.

### Scene 9 (58 words)
The four friends met on the sidewalk, each carrying their carefully chosen swap items. They walked together toward the community center as the morning sun warmed their backs. They chatted excitedly about what treasures they might discover. "I'm hoping someone brought books about painting techniques," Riley mentioned hopefully. The community center building appeared ahead of them, already looking busy.

### Scene 10 (61 words)
Tables filled every corner of the community center's main room when they arrived. Neighbors were arranging boxes of various items on every available surface. Mrs. Patterson stood near the main entrance, organizing the event with a clipboard and a welcoming smile. "Welcome to the swap meet!" she called out warmly. The room buzzed with activity and friendly conversation. The friends looked around at all the possibilities.

### Scene 11 (58 words)
Mrs. Patterson walked over to greet them personally. "The rule is simple," she explained with a friendly expression. "Fair trades only. If both people are satisfied with the exchange, then it's a successful swap." She gestured toward an empty table section near the window. "You can set up your items over there." The friends thanked her warmly and headed to the spot.

### Scene 12 (59 words)
They arranged their items neatly on their section of the table. The puzzle sat beside the colored pencils in an organized display. The book stood next to the toy car at an angle. The ball and drum completed their presentation. Ellis suggested organizing everything by category. Layla was already scanning other tables eagerly. "Let's see what everyone else brought!" she said with anticipation.

### Scene 13 (60 words)
The friends spread out to browse through the different tables. Various tables displayed old toys, picture books, kitchen items, and holiday decorations. One table offered board games with all pieces accounted for. Another presented gently used stuffed animals in good condition. The variety of items truly amazed them. People of all ages wandered between tables, examining items carefully and chatting warmly with their neighbors.

### Scene 14 (59 words)
Riley spotted something particularly special at a craft supply table across the room. An elderly woman with kind eyes sat behind a set of watercolor paints. The paints showed signs of use but clearly had plenty of color remaining. Riley's eyes sparkled with interest and hope. "Are you interested in trading for those?" the woman asked kindly. Riley nodded with enthusiasm.

### Scene 15 (58 words)
"What do you have to offer in exchange?" the woman asked pleasantly. Riley showed her the worn colored pencils and decorative stickers from her bag. The woman's face brightened with genuine delight. "My grandchildren absolutely love to color," she said warmly. "These pencils are perfect for their little hands." They exchanged items happily. Riley held the watercolors carefully, feeling grateful.

### Scene 16 (59 words)
The friends gathered back at their table to share discoveries from around the room. Riley displayed her new watercolors with obvious pride. Ellis had traded his book for a magnifying glass with a brass handle. A young boy walked past them, happily banging Benny's old toy drum. "My pencils were just right for her grandkids," Riley explained. "One person's old thing becomes another's treasure."

### Scene 17 (58 words)
Benny smiled broadly as he watched the young boy drum with pure joy. "I honestly forgot that drum was even in my closet," he said thoughtfully. "But just look at how happy he is!" The boy's enthusiastic drumming echoed cheerfully across the room. Something Benny had completely overlooked was bringing someone else such obvious happiness. That realization felt wonderful.

### Scene 18 (59 words)
A young boy and his parent approached their table with interest. The boy's eyes grew wide when he spotted Layla's puzzle on the display. "I've never seen this one before!" he said with obvious excitement. Layla smiled at his reaction. She had solved that same puzzle countless times, but for him, it represented a completely new challenge. She traded it for his kaleidoscope.

### Scene 19 (58 words)
The swap meet gradually wound down as afternoon arrived. The friends sat together on benches outside the community center entrance. Golden afternoon sunlight warmed their faces as they examined their new treasures. "We didn't spend a single dollar," Layla realized with wonder. "But everyone walked away with something they genuinely wanted." Ellis nodded thoughtfully. "Value truly depends on the person."

### Scene 20 (61 words)
Layla's mind started working as she processed the day's experiences. "Our Trading Post was just the four of us," she said slowly, building to something. "But this swap meet included the whole neighborhood." She looked at her friends with growing excitement in her expression. "What if we created something even bigger? Like a real market where everyone shares what they make?" The idea took root.
