# CHAPTER 1: THE SWAP MEET - TIER 3 (3RD GRADE)

**Reading Levels:** M-P
**Target Narration Speed:** 125 WPM
**Target Word Count:** 1,000 words
**Episode Duration:** ~8 minutes
**Character Focus:** All four friends working together
**FL Integration:** Fair trade, perceived value — what's "junk" to one is treasure to another
**Setting:** Neighborhood community center
**S1 Callback:** Reference to the Trading Post

## THE SWAP MEET

### Scene 1 (50 words)
Layla walked through the neighborhood on a sunny Saturday morning. The air felt warm and fresh. She noticed something colorful on a telephone pole ahead. As she got closer, she saw it was a hand-painted sign. The bright letters caught her attention immediately. She stopped to read what it said.

### Scene 2 (51 words)
"Community Swap Meet — Today!" the sign announced. Below that it said: "Bring what you don't need. Find what you do!" Layla read the time and place — ten o'clock at the community center. Her eyes lit up with excitement. "That sounds like our Trading Post, but even bigger!" she said aloud.

### Scene 3 (50 words)
Layla ran to Riley's house and knocked on the door. Riley answered, still wearing her pajamas. "You have to come see this!" Layla said breathlessly. She told Riley about the swap meet sign. Riley's face brightened immediately. "A swap meet? We should bring stuff to trade!" she said enthusiastically.

### Scene 4 (51 words)
The two friends hurried to the park to find Ellis and Benny. They were tossing a ball back and forth near the swings. "Guess what's happening at the community center today?" Layla called out. She explained the swap meet. Ellis looked interested. "What should we bring?" he asked thoughtfully. Everyone started planning.

### Scene 5 (50 words)
Layla went home to search her room. She opened her closet and looked through her toy box carefully. She found a puzzle she had completed many times before. The pieces were all there, but she knew every corner by heart. "Maybe someone else would enjoy figuring this out," she decided.

### Scene 6 (51 words)
Riley looked through her art supplies at home. She found some colored pencils that had gotten short from use. She also discovered stickers she no longer wanted to keep. "These pencils still work perfectly," she reasoned. "Someone might want them." She placed them in a small bag along with the stickers.

### Scene 7 (50 words)
Ellis organized his room before selecting items. He chose a book he had already read twice and understood well. He also found a toy car he had outgrown. He placed both items in a box. "I loved this book," he thought. "It's time for someone else to discover it."

### Scene 8 (51 words)
Benny dug through his belongings with enthusiasm. He found a bright bouncy ball and a small toy drum. He hadn't played with either in a long time. "I forgot I even had these!" he said cheerfully. He picked them up and added them to his pile. They were still perfectly good.

### Scene 9 (50 words)
The four friends met on the sidewalk, each carrying their swap items. They walked together toward the community center. The morning sun warmed their backs. They chatted excitedly about what they might find. "I hope someone has books about art," Riley said. The community center appeared ahead of them.

### Scene 10 (51 words)
Tables filled the community center's main room. Neighbors arranged boxes of items on every surface. Mrs. Patterson stood near the entrance, organizing everything with a friendly smile. "Welcome to the swap meet!" she called out. The room buzzed with activity and conversation. The friends looked around with wide eyes at all the possibilities.

### Scene 11 (50 words)
Mrs. Patterson walked over to greet them. "The rule is simple," she explained. "Fair trades. If both people are happy with the exchange, it's a good swap." She gestured toward an empty table section. "You can set up your items there." The friends thanked her and headed to the spot.

### Scene 12 (51 words)
They arranged their items neatly on the table. The puzzle sat next to the pencils. The book stood beside the toy car. The ball and drum completed the display. Ellis suggested organizing by type. Layla was already looking at other tables, eager to explore. "Let's see what everyone else brought!" she said.

### Scene 13 (50 words)
The friends spread out to browse. Tables displayed old toys, picture books, kitchen items, and decorations. One table had board games with all the pieces. Another offered gently used stuffed animals. The variety amazed them. People of all ages wandered between tables, examining items and talking with their neighbors happily.

### Scene 14 (51 words)
Riley spotted something special at a craft supply table. An elderly woman sat behind a set of watercolor paints. The paints were used but had plenty of color left. Riley's eyes sparkled. "Are you interested in trading for those?" the woman asked kindly. Riley nodded eagerly, her heart beating faster.

### Scene 15 (50 words)
"What do you have to offer?" the woman asked. Riley showed her the worn colored pencils and stickers. The woman smiled warmly. "My grandchildren love to color," she said. "These pencils are perfect for little hands." They exchanged items happily. Riley couldn't believe it — real watercolors were now hers!

### Scene 16 (51 words)
The friends gathered back at their table to share discoveries. Riley showed her watercolors proudly. Ellis had traded his book for a magnifying glass. Benny watched a young boy walk away, happily banging the toy drum. "My pencils were perfect for her grandkids," Riley explained. "One person's old thing is another's treasure."

### Scene 17 (50 words)
Benny smiled as he watched the boy with his former drum. "I forgot that drum was even in my room," he said thoughtfully. "But listen how happy he is!" The boy's joyful drumming echoed across the room. Something Benny had ignored brought someone else so much happiness. That felt wonderful.

### Scene 18 (51 words)
A young boy and his parent approached their table. The boy's eyes grew wide when he saw Layla's puzzle. "I've never seen this one before!" he said excitedly. Layla smiled. She had solved that puzzle countless times, but for him, it was completely new. She agreed to trade for his kaleidoscope.

### Scene 19 (50 words)
The swap meet wound down as afternoon arrived. The friends sat together on benches outside the community center. Golden sunlight warmed them as they examined their new treasures. "We didn't spend any money," Layla realized. "But everyone got something they wanted." Ellis nodded thoughtfully. "Value isn't about the thing itself."

### Scene 20 (52 words)
Layla's mind started working. "Our Trading Post was just the four of us," she said slowly. "But this was the whole neighborhood." She looked at her friends with growing excitement. "What if we did something even bigger? Like a real market where everyone shares what they make and learn?" The idea took root.
