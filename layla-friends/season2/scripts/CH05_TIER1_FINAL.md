## THE SPICE MARKET

### Scene 1 (31 words)
The friends sat in the attic. Mr. Mason held a small pouch. It smelled like spices. "Let me take you to Morocco," he said. Everyone got quiet. They were ready to listen.

### Scene 2 (30 words)
The attic started to fade. Colors got brighter. They could see a big market. There were stalls everywhere. People walked and talked. It was loud and busy. Morocco!

### Scene 3 (31 words)
A young man walked into the market. It was Mr. Mason! He was young. He had dark hair. His eyes were wide. "So many colors," he thought. "So many smells."

### Scene 4 (30 words)
Young Mr. Mason had a job. He needed to find saffron. His mama asked for it. "Get the good stuff," she said. He wanted to make her happy.

### Scene 5 (31 words)
He found the spice stalls. There were piles of spices. Red ones. Gold ones. Orange ones. They looked like little mountains. "So pretty," he said. He needed to pick one.

### Scene 6 (30 words)
A man ran up to him. "You buy! Best price!" the man yelled. He was too close. Young Mr. Mason stepped back. He felt pushed. He did not like it.

### Scene 7 (31 words)
Young Mr. Mason walked away. He felt bad. He wanted to buy. But he did not like being rushed. "What is the right price?" he asked himself. He did not know.

### Scene 8 (30 words)
Then he saw a quiet stall. An old man sat there. His name was Hassan. He smiled. He did not yell. "Take your time," Hassan said. His voice was calm.

### Scene 9 (31 words)
Young Mr. Mason stopped. "I need saffron," he said. "It is for my mama." Hassan nodded. "What will she make?" he asked. "Rice," said Mr. Mason. "She makes good rice."

### Scene 10 (32 words)
Hassan showed him three kinds of saffron. "This one is good," he said. "This one is better. And this one is the best." He held up the red threads. They were pretty.

### Scene 11 (30 words)
Hassan said the price. It was more than Mr. Mason thought. He felt worried. "That is a lot," he said softly. Hassan smiled. "Tell me what you can pay."

### Scene 12 (31 words)
"A deal is not a fight," Hassan said. "It is a talk. You say what you can pay. I say what I need. We find the middle. Both of us smile."

### Scene 13 (30 words)
They talked more. Young Mr. Mason said a lower price. Hassan came down a little. They found a price they both liked. They shook hands. Both of them smiled.

### Scene 14 (31 words)
Hassan wrapped the saffron in paper. He put it in a small pouch. He gave it to Mr. Mason. "Take this to your mama," he said. "It came with respect."

### Scene 15 (30 words)
"One more thing," Hassan said. "Never rush a good thing. Patience shows you care." Young Mr. Mason nodded. He would remember this. Patience was important. He learned that today.

### Scene 16 (31 words)
Young Mr. Mason walked through the market. But now he walked slowly. He looked at things. He smiled. He held the pouch tight. He felt different. He felt calm and happy.

### Scene 17 (30 words)
The market started to fade. The colors got soft. They were back in the attic. Mr. Mason was old again. He still held the pouch. The same spice pouch.

### Scene 18 (31 words)
The friends looked at Mr. Mason. Their eyes were big. "Wow," said Riley. "I want to see a market like that!" Mr. Mason smiled. He put the pouch in the trunk.

### Scene 19 (30 words)
Layla was quiet. She was thinking. She always wanted to do things fast. Maybe that was not good. "I rush too much," she said softly. "I need to listen more."

### Scene 20 (32 words)
The friends looked at each other. They would have their own market soon. "We need fair prices," said Ellis. "Prices that make everyone happy." Layla nodded. "And we cannot rush people."
