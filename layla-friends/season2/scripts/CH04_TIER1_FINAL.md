## MR. MASON'S TRUNK

### Scene 1 (30 words)
The friends went to Mr. Mason's house. He was sitting on his porch. "Hello!" he said. "I need help in my attic. Can you help me reach high things?"

### Scene 2 (31 words)
They went inside his house. It was cozy and warm. There were photos on the walls. There were maps and little things from far away. "You have so many cool things!" said Riley happily.

### Scene 3 (30 words)
Mr. Mason took them to a ladder. It went up to the attic. Dust floated in the light. "I haven't been up here in a long time," he said. "Let's go up!"

### Scene 4 (30 words)
The attic was full of boxes. Old things were everywhere. Sunlight came through a little window. "Wow!" said Layla. "It's like a museum!" The friends looked around with big eyes.

### Scene 5 (31 words)
They helped Mr. Mason clean. He told them what to keep and what to throw away. "That can stay. That can go." It was normal work. The friends moved boxes around carefully.

### Scene 6 (30 words)
Ellis found something in the corner. It was a big old trunk! It had stickers from all over the world. "Mr. Mason!" said Ellis. "What is this big thing?"

### Scene 7 (30 words)
Everyone came to look. Mr. Mason's face got soft and happy. He touched the trunk gently. "My old traveling trunk," he said. "It went everywhere with me when I was young."

### Scene 8 (31 words)
Mr. Mason opened the trunk slowly. Inside were wrapped objects. There were old photos too. "Every object in here is a memory," he said. "Each one has a story." The friends leaned closer.

### Scene 9 (30 words)
Mr. Mason took out a small bag. It had spices inside. He smelled them and smiled. "This is from Morocco," he said. "I learned to make deals there." It smelled nice.

### Scene 10 (31 words)
Mr. Mason's eyes looked far away. He was remembering. "There were stalls everywhere," he said. "Spices in every color. Men selling things and calling out." The friends wanted to hear more.

### Scene 11 (30 words)
"You can look inside," said Mr. Mason. "But be careful. These things are special." The friends looked in the trunk. They found many interesting objects from around the whole world.

### Scene 12 (30 words)
Riley found a little wooden fish. "That's from Japan," said Mr. Mason. "I saw a fish sale there. I learned about prices." Riley held the fish gently in her hands.

### Scene 13 (31 words)
Benny found a wooden stamp with pretty patterns. "That's for making bread," said Mr. Mason. "It's from Italy. A family bakery." Benny turned it over and looked at the designs carefully.

### Scene 14 (30 words)
Ellis found a tiny net. "That's from a fishing village," said Mr. Mason. "I helped sell fish there one morning. I learned that some things need to sell fast."

### Scene 15 (30 words)
Layla found a wooden star. It was very pretty. "That's from Germany," said Mr. Mason. "From a Christmas market. Snow and lights and handmade gifts." Layla held it up.

### Scene 16 (31 words)
Mr. Mason looked at the friends. "These are not just old things," he said. "They have lessons inside. I was waiting to share them with the right people. I think that's you."

### Scene 17 (30 words)
The friends got excited. "Will you tell us the stories?" asked Layla. "Please!" said Benny. They all wanted to hear about the things in the trunk. Every object had a lesson.

### Scene 18 (30 words)
Mr. Mason smiled. "One story at a time," he said. He held up the spice bag. "Let's start with Morocco. That's where our journey begins." The friends got comfortable.

### Scene 19 (30 words)
Mr. Mason started to talk about Morocco. Something magical seemed to happen. The attic felt different. Colors and sounds seemed to appear. The story was coming alive around them all!

### Scene 20 (31 words)
The friends sat around the trunk. They were ready to learn. Mr. Mason would teach them about markets around the world. And Layla thought: "This will help us make our own market!"
