# CHAPTER 1: THE SWAP MEET - TIER 2 (2ND GRADE)

**Reading Levels:** J-M
**Target Narration Speed:** 112 WPM
**Target Word Count:** 900 words
**Episode Duration:** ~8 minutes
**Character Focus:** All four friends working together
**FL Integration:** Fair trade, perceived value — what's "junk" to one is treasure to another
**Setting:** Neighborhood community center
**S1 Callback:** Reference to the Trading Post

---

## THE SWAP MEET

### Scene 1 (45 words)
Layla walked through the neighborhood on a sunny Saturday morning. The warm air felt nice on her face. She noticed something colorful on a telephone pole up ahead. As she got closer, she saw it was a sign with big painted letters.

### Scene 2 (46 words)
The sign said "Community Swap Meet — Today!" Layla read the rest: "Bring what you don't need. Find what you do!" Her eyes grew wide with excitement. "That sounds like our Trading Post, but way bigger!" she said out loud. She had to tell her friends.

### Scene 3 (45 words)
Layla ran to Riley's house and knocked quickly on the door. Riley answered, still in her pajamas. "You need to see this!" Layla said excitedly. She told Riley about the swap meet. Riley's whole face lit up. "We should bring stuff to trade!"

### Scene 4 (46 words)
They hurried to the park to find Ellis and Benny. The boys were tossing a ball near the swings. "There's a swap meet today at the community center!" Layla announced. She explained how it worked. Ellis looked interested. "What should we bring?" he asked. Everyone started making plans.

### Scene 5 (45 words)
Layla went home to look through her things. She searched her closet and her toy box. She found a puzzle she had done many times before. Every piece was familiar now. "Maybe someone else would love to figure this out," she thought. She set it aside.

### Scene 6 (46 words)
Riley went through her art supplies carefully. She found colored pencils that had gotten short from use. She also found stickers she didn't need anymore. "These pencils still work great," she said. "Someone else might really want them." She put everything in a small bag.

### Scene 7 (45 words)
Ellis picked items from his neat room. He chose a book he had read twice already. He also found a toy car he never played with anymore. He put them in a box. "I know this book by heart now," he thought. "Time for someone new."

### Scene 8 (46 words)
Benny searched through his stuff with excitement. He found a bright bouncy ball and a small toy drum. He had forgotten about both of them. "I haven't touched these in forever!" he said cheerfully. He grabbed them happily. They were still in perfect shape.

### Scene 9 (45 words)
The four friends met on the sidewalk. Each one carried their swap items. They walked together toward the community center. The morning sun warmed their backs. They chatted about what they hoped to find. "Maybe someone has art books," Riley said hopefully. The building appeared ahead.

### Scene 10 (46 words)
Inside the community center, tables filled the main room. Neighbors were arranging boxes of items everywhere. Mrs. Patterson stood near the entrance with a clipboard. "Welcome to the swap meet!" she called out warmly. The room was busy and full of energy. The friends looked around in amazement.

### Scene 11 (46 words)
Mrs. Patterson walked over to greet them. "The rule is simple," she explained with a smile. "Fair trades. If both people are happy, it's a good swap." She pointed to an empty table nearby. "Set up your items there." The friends thanked her and walked over together.

### Scene 12 (46 words)
They arranged everything neatly on the table. The puzzle sat next to the colored pencils. The book stood beside the toy car. The ball and drum finished the display. Ellis organized them by type. Layla was already looking at other tables. "Let's explore!" she said eagerly.

### Scene 13 (45 words)
The friends walked around the room to browse. Tables displayed old toys, picture books, games, and kitchen items. One table had puzzles with all the pieces. Another had gently used stuffed animals. So many interesting things! People of all ages wandered around, chatting and smiling.

### Scene 14 (46 words)
Riley spotted something wonderful at a craft supply table. An elderly woman sat behind a set of watercolor paints. The paints were used but still had lots of color. Riley's eyes sparkled. "Would you like to trade for those?" the woman asked kindly. Riley nodded eagerly.

### Scene 15 (46 words)
"What do you have to offer?" the woman asked. Riley showed her the worn colored pencils and stickers. The woman's face brightened. "My grandchildren love to color!" she said happily. "These are just right for little hands." They traded items. Riley held the paints with joy.

### Scene 16 (46 words)
The friends met back at their table. Riley showed off her new watercolors proudly. Ellis had traded his book for a magnifying glass. Nearby, a young boy walked past banging Benny's old drum happily. "My old pencils were perfect for her grandkids," Riley said. "Amazing how that works!"

### Scene 17 (46 words)
Benny watched the boy drumming with a big smile. "I totally forgot about that drum," he said thoughtfully. "But look how much fun he's having!" The joyful sound of drumming filled the room. Something Benny had forgotten about was making someone else really happy.

### Scene 18 (46 words)
A young boy and his mom came to their table. The boy spotted Layla's puzzle. "I've never seen this one!" he said excitedly. Layla smiled. She had solved it so many times. But for this boy, it was brand new! She traded it for his kaleidoscope.

### Scene 19 (46 words)
The swap meet was ending. The friends sat on benches outside in the afternoon sun. They looked at the new things they had found. "We didn't spend any money," Layla said with wonder. "But everyone got something they wanted." Ellis nodded. "Value is different for everyone."

### Scene 20 (46 words)
Layla's mind started racing. "Our Trading Post was just the four of us," she said slowly. "But this was the whole neighborhood!" She looked at her friends with excitement building. "What if we made something even bigger?" The idea was beginning to grow in all of them.
