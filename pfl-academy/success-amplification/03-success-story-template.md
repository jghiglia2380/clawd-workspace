# Success Story Template
## Case Study Format for Instructors & Schools

### Purpose
Provide a replicable template for creating compelling success stories that instructors can share with their administration and PFL Academy can use for marketing.

---

## Story Structure: The Narrative Arc

Every success story follows this structure:

1. **The Context** — Who, where, what situation
2. **The Challenge** — What problem they faced
3. **The Approach** — How they used PFL Academy
4. **The Results** — What happened (with numbers)
5. **The Impact** — Broader implications, quotes
6. **The Takeaway** — What others can learn

## Example: Dianna Martinez Success Story

*(To be completed with real data from Dianna)*

# How Dianna Martinez at [School Name] Transformed Her Business Class Into a Startup Incubator

**Location:** [City], California
**Grade Level:** 11th-12th Grade
**Subject:** Business / Entrepreneurship
**Time Using PFL Academy:** [X] semesters

---

### THE CONTEXT

Dianna Martinez teaches business and entrepreneurship at [School Name] in [City], California. With a passion for giving students real-world skills, she was looking for curriculum that went beyond textbook theory.

---

### THE CHALLENGE

**Before PFL Academy:**
- Students learned financial concepts in isolation, without seeing how they connect
- Limited time and resources to create authentic business development experiences
- Needed engaging content that met state CTE standards while keeping students interested

> "I wanted my students to actually *build* something, not just read about entrepreneurship. But creating that kind of curriculum from scratch? I didn't have the time or resources."

---

### THE APPROACH

Dianna implemented PFL Academy's full curriculum, structuring her semester around the chapter progression. She supplemented the digital content with weekly "pitch sessions" where students presented their evolving business ideas to peers.

**Implementation highlights:**
- Used the simulation games as warm-up activities
- Had students complete business plans in teams of 2-3
- Invited local business owners for a final pitch competition
- Integrated financial literacy chapters with math department

---

### THE RESULTS

**By the Numbers:**

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Students Reached | [X] students |
| Business Plans Created | [X] plans |
| Completion Rate | [X%] |
| Average Assessment Score | [X%] |
| Total Projected Revenue | $[X] across all student plans |

**Standout Achievements:**
- [X] students presented at district showcase
- One student team actually launched their business (selling at local farmers market)
- Dianna received [recognition] from administration

---

### THE IMPACT

**For Students:**
> "I never thought I could actually start a business. Now I have a real plan, and I'm doing it this summer."
> — [Student Name], 12th Grade

Students left the class not just with financial knowledge, but with confidence and tangible work products for college applications.

**For the Instructor:**
> "My principal actually asked me to present at our district PD day. That's never happened before. PFL Academy made me look good because my students were producing real work."

Dianna has since been recognized as a leader in CTE at her school.

**For the School/Administration:**
The program helped the school meet state CTE requirements while also generating positive parent feedback and visible student outcomes.

---

### THE TAKEAWAY

**Key lessons:**
1. Structure matters — following the chapter progression creates natural momentum
2. Add a social element — peer presentations increased engagement
3. Invite outside voices — local business owners validated the experience for students

> "If you're on the fence, just start. The curriculum does the heavy lifting. Your job is just to facilitate and watch them surprise you."

## Template Variations

### Short Version (For Newsletter/Email)
- 150-200 words
- Focus on one key result
- One quote
- One CTA

### Social Media Version
- 2-3 sentences + key stat
- Image of instructor or student work
- Link to full story

### Video Script Version
- 2-3 minute interview format
- Same structure but conversational
- B-roll suggestions included

## Data Collection Questions

*Use these when interviewing instructors for success stories:*

### Context
1. Tell me about your school and students.
2. What subject/class do you use PFL Academy in?
3. How long have you been using it?

### Challenge
4. What were you using before PFL Academy?
5. What problems were you trying to solve?
6. What was missing in your previous approach?

### Approach
7. How do you structure your use of PFL Academy?
8. Any creative adaptations you've made?
9. What features do you use most?

### Results
10. How many students have gone through the program?
11. What's your completion rate?
12. Any standout student projects?
13. Have any students actually started businesses?

### Impact
14. How has this affected your students beyond grades?
15. Any recognition from administration?
16. What do parents say?
17. Would you recommend PFL Academy to colleagues?

### Advice
18. What would you tell another teacher considering this?
19. Any tips for implementation?
20. What surprised you most?

## Review Checklist

Before publishing any success story:

- [ ] Instructor has reviewed and approved
- [ ] All quotes are accurate and attributed
- [ ] Numbers are verified
- [ ] School name usage approved
- [ ] Student names/quotes have consent (if used)
- [ ] Photos have permission
- [ ] Links work
- [ ] CTA is appropriate for channel
